Prototype

Episerver - Commerce order manager

2017

Challenge

Towards the end of my 8 year tenure at Episerver, I was asked to develop a prototype for the order management system of the e-commerce product.

Being a legacy area of the product which hadn't been touched for far too long, we saw the opportunity to significantly improve the quality of life of the Customer Service Representatives (CSR) who would be using the product.

The purpose of the prototype was to explore possibilities, and test assumptions to really hone in on the workflow of the CSR. As such, it was designed only for desktop, with speed and agility in mind.

Compatibility

This is a prototype and has only been tested to work in Chrome or Firefox.

About Episerver

Episerver is Sweden's leading CMS, E-Commerce and personalisation platform.

My role

I designed everything in the browser, and built a very high fidelity prototype using Mockaroo for large and deep data sets.

The prototype was developed over a couple of weeks with frequent check-ins with Directors of Product David Bowen and Christian Schlosser.

Discovery

Interviewing CSR's, looking at our own system as well as those of competitors, it was clear that speed and efficiency was of the essence.

CSR's are often measured by their efficiency, and number of issues handled. Instead of having multiple different pages supporting individual functions, we wanted to bring it all together into one workflow. Primary features like updating a customers information, as well as CRUD'ing (Create, Remove, Update, Delete pattern) orders should be quickly accessible in the same context.

We also had to make sure the prototype works for both B2C and B2B.
