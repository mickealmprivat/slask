Case study

Mr Green - Swedish deposit limits (version 2)

2019

Problem description

In January of 2019, Sweden became a regulated gambling market under the Swedish Gambling Authority (Spelinspektionen), putting several responsible gambling laws in place. One of these requires all new players to set deposit limits when they register a new account, which is great as it forces the player to think about their gambling habits and hopefully leads to greater player protection.

A few months after our initial release, it became clear that players were setting incorrect limits, leading to lots of player frustration, customer support contacts, and loss of revenue.

User journey

At this point, the user has just completed the registration process, and we must now present them with quite a bit of information to digest, on top of forcing more input fields on them.

Discovery

Our initial implementation was delivered under a tight deadline without testing, so we welcomed this opportunity to return and make improvements on it. The controls we had used were copied from an existing implementation on the site, and felt cumbersome and lacked any kind of anchoring for new players.

Digging further into the problem, we looked into BI-Data, Voice of Customer reports, and interviewed staff in Customer Service to get a better understanding of what the problem space might be. We deduced that players (especially first time players) had difficulty estimating what a reasonable limit would be, as well as understanding how the different limits relate to each other and when they are reached/reset.

We then had two different problem areas to start focusing on: improving the flow of setting limits, and increasing player understanding of their mechanics.

Limit setting

We needed to improve the flow of setting the actual limits.

Onboarding

Players setting limits for themselves that are correct and realistic is crucial both for playing responsibly, and taking the rest of tools we provide seriously. They can be very difficult to grasp, however, and a major problem we noticed from both BI-data and customer support feedback was players simply having set the wrong limits due to lack of understanding.

Having just come from the registration flow, I felt we had an opportunity to try and "lighten things up" and give the user a bit of a break before proceeding to the next set of input controls.

Handover

The best developer hand-over for animation is almost always in code, rather than a video produced in Studio or After Effects. Instead of having to document easings and timings, it's right there on the page for them. So I brought up a new Pen and produced the animation to be easily adapted to whatever triggers our code stack might provide.

Usability testing

Before releasing to market we did usability testing on the entire flow using our eyetracker from Tobii, generating a 100% completion rate, and a subjective feeling of "that was easy!"
